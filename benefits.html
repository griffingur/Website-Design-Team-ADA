Benefits

Bio-Friendly homes have a large amount of benefits.

The biggest benefits of using solar and wind energy are:

  • Renewable Energy Source- Solar and wind energy are available across the world every day and we will never run out of it as long as we have the sun.

  • Reduces Electricity Bills- Using solar and wind energy takes a huge chunk out of energy bill and you can also receive payments for the surplus energy you export back to the grid if you generate more electricity than you use.

  • Diverse Applications- You can use solar energy to generate electricity or heat. Solar energy can be used to produce electricity in areas without access to the energy grid. Solar energy can be put into materials used for buildings such as windows or roofs.

  • Low Maintenance Costs- Solar and wind energy systems generally do not need much maintenance or repairs as long as you keep them clean to keep them most efficient.

  • Better For The Earth- Solar and wind energy generate electricity and heat without burning fossil fuels so it doesn’t pollute the air or have any harmful emissions that go into our atmosphere.

Features

The features that come with the application of a bio-friendly home.

Some features of Bio-Friendly homes are:

  • Solar Panels- Converts sunlight into electricity and or heat.

  • Energy Star appliances- Energy Star appliances are up to 40% more efficient than standard appliances.

  • High Efficiency, Water Conserving Appliances- Cut down water usage by using low flow showerheads, faucets, and toilets.

  • Dual Pane, Low-Emittance Windows- Low emittance windows are great insulation and also block UV rays which can damage belongings in your home.

  • Energy Efficient Lighting- Light dimmer switches are 10% more efficient than just off/on switches, and you can also use sensor light switches that will turn off the lights if no one is in the room. Compact Fluorescent Lights are the most efficient light bulb.

  • Certified Wood from Sustainable Forests- Using building materials from forests where trees are constantly replaced helps conserve forests that are being cut down.

  • Blown-in Cellulose Wall Insulation- Most homes have sheets of insulation built-in, but using blown-in insulation more efficiently fills in all the gaps, and also cellulose is non-toxic and can increase your home’s fire resistance from 22 to 57%.
